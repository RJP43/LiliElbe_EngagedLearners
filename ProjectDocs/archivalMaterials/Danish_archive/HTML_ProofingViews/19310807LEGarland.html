(#d02) "Kære Veninde"

(#d03 #d04 #d05 #d06) Tusind Tak for Brevet! Jeg talte imorges med Professoren om Bogen. Han synes, det er udmærket altsammen – og har kun nogle rent videnskabelige Indvendinger, over for hvad jeg Editor: har misforstaaet. Snarest muligt giver han Besked derom. Men han er ikke slem til at skrive Breve – gør det næsten aldrig – og han har saa traveltTranslator: i disse Dage, fordi han i Begyndelsen af næste Uge tager Ferie. Tænk, jeg har endnu ikke faaet Lov til at komme op. Men nu kan det ikke være længe, inden jeg atter er paa Benene.

(#d02) Dear Friend

(#d03 #do4 #d05 #d06) A thousand thanks for the letter! I spoke this morning with the professor about the book. He thinks everything is very good – and only has some purely scientific objections about what I have misunderstood. As soon as possible he will let me know about it. But he is not the best at writing letters – he almost never does it – and he is so busy these days because he is taking vacation at the beginning of next week. Can you believe, I have not yet gotten permission to get up. But it cannot be long before I am on my feet again.

(#d07 #d08) A thousand hearty greetings to You and Your Husband Your
Lili Elvenes
